New Football Pitch Inaugurated in Mwala

Community partnership leads to the development of a safe play environment for youth in Machakos County, providing a dedicated space for sports and community gatherings.

The Nzoka Kivuva Foundation, in partnership with the Mwala community and the Machakos County Government, officially inaugurated a new football pitch in Mwala, Machakos County. The facility provides a safe and accessible play environment for over 200 young people in the area, serving as a hub for sports, recreation, and community engagement.

The pitch, which was developed on land provided by the community, features a full-sized football field with goal posts, perimeter fencing, and basic spectator facilities. The project was funded through a combination of community contributions, foundation resources, and county government support.

Community members and officials at the inauguration of the new football pitch in Mwala

A Safe Space for Youth Development

The new pitch is more than just a sports facility — it's a symbol of community partnership and investment in youth. For years, young people in Mwala had limited access to safe play spaces, forcing them to practice on uneven or unsafe grounds. The new facility changes that, providing a dedicated space where young athletes can develop their skills in a safe and supportive environment.

  • Full-sized football pitch for matches and training
  • Perimeter fencing for safety and security
  • Spectator area for community gatherings
  • Landscaped surroundings for a clean environment
  • Access to water for maintenance

Community Partnership in Action

The project is a shining example of what can be achieved when communities, government, and organizations work together. Local residents contributed their time and labor, the county government provided technical support, and the foundation coordinated resources and funding.

"This pitch belongs to the community," said Advocate Nzoka Kivuva, Executive Director of the foundation. "We simply helped make it happen. The real heroes are the community members who came together to create something lasting for their children."

Young people enjoying the new football pitch in Mwala
Community celebration at the pitch inauguration

Impact on the Community

The new pitch has already begun to transform the community. In the weeks since its inauguration, it has become a hub for youth activities, hosting football matches, community events, and youth development programs. The facility has also created opportunities for local coaches to run training sessions and for young referees to gain experience.

"The pitch has brought our community together," said a local coach. "We now have a place where we can train our young players properly, and they're already showing improvement. It's making a real difference."

What's Next

Building on the success of this project, the foundation and community partners are planning to:

  • Install floodlights for evening training
  • Develop a youth league using the facility
  • Create a community sports committee for sustainability
  • Expand the project to other communities in Machakos County

"This is just the beginning," said the foundation's program manager. "We're already in discussions with other communities who want to replicate this model. Our goal is to ensure that every young person in Machakos County has access to a safe, quality sports facility."
