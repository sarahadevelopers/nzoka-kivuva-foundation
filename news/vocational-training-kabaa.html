Vocational Training Program Expands to Kabaa

Youth gain practical skills in tailoring, carpentry, and agribusiness through partnerships with local vocational institutions in Kabaa, Machakos County.

The Nzoka Kivuva Foundation has expanded its vocational training program to Kabaa, equipping 60 young people with practical skills in tailoring, carpentry, and agribusiness. The initiative, launched in partnership with local vocational institutions and community leaders, aims to improve the employability and entrepreneurial potential of youth in the region.

The program provides hands-on training, mentorship, and access to tools and materials, enabling participants to develop marketable skills that can lead to sustainable livelihoods.

Youth participating in vocational training sessions in Kabaa

Building Skills, Building Futures

The vocational training program offers participants hands-on experience in three key areas:

  • Tailoring and Fashion Design – Garment making, repairs, and design
  • Carpentry and Woodwork – Furniture making and joinery
  • Agribusiness – Sustainable farming, crop production, and livestock management

Empowering Youth for Economic Independence

The program is designed to address high unemployment rates among young people in Machakos County by providing them with skills that are in demand in the local economy. Participants receive not only technical training but also entrepreneurship and business management skills to help them start their own enterprises.

"We believe that every young person deserves the opportunity to build a better life," said Advocate Nzoka Kivuva, Executive Director. "Vocational training is a powerful tool for empowerment. It gives young people the skills they need to earn an income, support their families, and contribute to their communities."

Students learning tailoring and fashion design skills
Participants learning sustainable farming techniques

A Collaborative Approach

The program's success is built on strong partnerships with local institutions, including:

  • Kabaa Vocational Training Center – Tailoring and fashion
  • Machakos Technical Institute – Carpentry and woodwork
  • Kabaa Agricultural Training Center – Agribusiness
  • Local Community Organizations – Mentorship and support

"This program is a true collaboration," said the foundation's program coordinator. "We're working with local institutions that have deep roots in the community. Together, we're creating opportunities that will have a lasting impact."

What's Next

Building on the success of the Kabaa expansion, the foundation plans to:

  • Extend the program to other sub-counties in Machakos
  • Offer advanced training and specialization
  • Establish a graduate support network
  • Provide startup capital for graduates to begin their own businesses

"We've seen incredible enthusiasm from the youth in Kabaa," said the program manager. "Many of them are already applying their skills to start small businesses. This is the kind of transformation we want to see across Machakos County."
