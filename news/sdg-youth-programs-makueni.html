Think Global, Act Local: How Our Youth Programs Achieve SDG 4 and SDG 8 in Machakos

Discover how the Nzoka Kivuva Foundation's youth programs in Machakos County are directly contributing to the UN Sustainable Development Goals — proving that local action creates global impact.

The United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a global blueprint for peace and prosperity. But they aren't just abstract targets for governments and international organizations — they are being achieved every day at the community level, by foundations like the Nzoka Kivuva Foundation.

In Machakos County, our youth programs are making direct contributions to two of the most critical SDGs: SDG 4 (Quality Education) and SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth). Here's how we're turning global goals into local impact.

"We believe that the SDGs are achievable when communities take ownership of them. Our work in Machakos is proof that local action creates global impact." — Advocate Nzioka Kivuva, Executive Director
Youth empowerment programs contributing to sustainable development goals

What Are the SDGs and Why Do They Matter?

The Sustainable Development Goals are a set of 17 interconnected goals adopted by all UN member states in 2015. They are designed to address the world's most pressing challenges, including poverty, inequality, climate change, and environmental degradation.

For NGOs and foundations in Kenya, the SDGs provide a roadmap for impact and a framework for measuring progress. Donors and international partners increasingly look for organizations that can demonstrate how their work contributes to the SDGs.

SDG 4: Quality Education

SDG 4 aims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all. At the Nzoka Kivuva Foundation, we are contributing to this goal through:

  • Sports-Linked Scholarships – Keeping vulnerable students in school
  • Learning Materials – Providing books, uniforms, and equipment
  • Mentorship Programs – Supporting students through their academic journey
  • Digital Literacy – Preparing youth for the 21st-century economy

"Every child deserves access to quality education. Our scholarship and mentorship programs are helping to ensure that young people in Machakos have the opportunities they need to succeed."

SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth

SDG 8 aims to promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all. We are contributing to this goal through:

  • Vocational Training – Equipping youth with practical, marketable skills
  • Digital Skills Programs – Preparing youth for remote work and the digital economy
  • Entrepreneurship Training – Supporting youth to start their own businesses
  • Sports-Based Livelihoods – Creating pathways to employment through sports

"We believe that decent work is the pathway out of poverty. By equipping youth with practical skills, we're helping them build sustainable livelihoods and contribute to the economic growth of Machakos County."

Real Stories, Real Impact

Case Study: SDG 4 in Action

Sarah Muthoni, a Form 3 student from Wote, was on the verge of dropping out of school when she received a sports-linked scholarship from the foundation. Today, she is not only back in school but is also an active member of her school's football team.

"I never thought I could afford to continue my education. This scholarship has given me hope and a future. I want to become a teacher and inspire other young people." — Sarah Muthoni, Scholarship Recipient

Case Study: SDG 8 in Action

David Mwangi, a 24-year-old from Kibwezi, was struggling to find employment after dropping out of school. Through our vocational training program, he gained skills in carpentry and woodwork.

"I never thought I could build a career without a university degree. Today, I'm a skilled carpenter, earning a steady income and supporting my family. Vocational training gave me a second chance." — David Mwangi, Carpentry Graduate

Today, David runs his own carpentry workshop, employing three other young people from his community — a testament to how local action can achieve global goals.

The Road Ahead

The Nzoka Kivuva Foundation is committed to deepening our contribution to the SDGs. In the coming years, we plan to:

  • Expand our scholarship program to support 100+ students annually
  • Establish a dedicated vocational training center in Machakos
  • Create a youth entrepreneurship fund to support new businesses
  • Develop a digital literacy program reaching 500+ youth
  • Build partnerships with international organizations aligned with the SDGs

"The SDGs provide a powerful framework for our work," said the foundation's program coordinator. "By aligning our programs with these global goals, we're able to demonstrate our impact to donors and partners, and we're ensuring that our work contributes to a better, more sustainable world."
