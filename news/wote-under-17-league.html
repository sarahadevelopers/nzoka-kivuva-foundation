Wote Under-17 League: A New Chapter for Machakos Youth

The Nzoka Kivuva Foundation officially launched the Wote Under-17 Football League, bringing together 12 teams from across Machakos County to foster unity, discipline, and talent development.

The Nzoka Kivuva Foundation officially launched the Wote Under-17 Football League on June 15, 2026, at the Wote Stadium. The tournament brings together 12 teams from across Machakos County, providing a structured platform for young athletes to compete, develop their skills, and build lasting friendships.

The league is part of the foundation's broader commitment to grassroots sports development, aimed at fostering unity, discipline, and talent development while keeping young people engaged in positive activities.

Players and officials at the opening ceremony of the Wote Under-17 Football League

Uniting Communities Through Football

The Wote Under-17 League is more than just a football tournament — it's a community-building initiative that brings together young people from different backgrounds, schools, and communities across Machakos County.

The league features teams from across the county, including:

  • Wote Youth FC
  • Makindu Stars
  • Kibwezi United
  • Kathonzweni All-Stars
  • Mbooni Warriors
  • Kilungu Rangers
  • Emali Youth
  • Sultan Hamud FC
  • Wote Academy
  • Makindu Young Stars
  • Kibwezi Eagles
  • Kathonzweni Lions
12 Teams Competing
240+ Players Registered
24 Matches Scheduled
10 Weeks Duration
"This league is a dream come true for these young players. Many of them have never had the opportunity to compete in an organized tournament before. The excitement and energy are incredible." — Community Sports Organizer, Wote

Developing Talent, Building Character

The league is designed to provide young athletes with a professional yet nurturing environment where they can develop their football skills while also learning important life lessons about teamwork, discipline, and fair play.

"We believe that sports is a powerful tool for youth development," said Advocate Nzioka Kivuva, Executive Director of the foundation. "Through this league, we're not just developing football talent — we're building character, instilling discipline, and creating a sense of belonging that will stay with these young people for the rest of their lives."

Intense match action during the league tournament
Players celebrating a goal with their teammates

A Long-Term Commitment to Youth Sports

The Wote Under-17 League is part of the foundation's long-term strategy to build a sustainable sports ecosystem in Machakos County. The foundation is providing teams with equipment, coaching support, and a safe environment to play and grow.

"This is just the beginning," said the league coordinator. "We're already planning to expand the league to include more teams and age categories in the coming years. Our vision is to create a pipeline of talent that can compete at the highest levels."

What's Next

Following the successful launch of the league, the foundation plans to:

  • Expand to 16 teams in 2027
  • Introduce an Under-15 category
  • Establish a talent identification program
  • Partner with professional clubs for pathways

"We're committed to making this league a permanent fixture in Machakos County," said the foundation's program manager. "We want to see these young athletes thrive, and we'll continue to invest in their development."
