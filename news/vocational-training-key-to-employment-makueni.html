The Alternative Path: Why Vocational Training Holds the Key to Makueni's Youth Employment

Discover why vocational training is the key to youth employment in Makueni County — and how the Nzoka Kivuva Foundation is equipping young people with practical skills for a brighter future.

For decades, the path to success in Kenya has been a straight line: go to school, get good grades, go to university, and find a white-collar job. But for millions of young people in rural areas like Makueni, this path is increasingly out of reach.

With youth unemployment in Kenya at over 15%, and even higher rates in rural areas, it's clear that the traditional academic model is no longer sufficient. The Nzoka Kivuva Foundation is championing an alternative path — one that leads to practical skills, technical expertise, and sustainable livelihoods.

"I never thought I could build a career without a university degree. Today, I'm a skilled carpenter, earning a steady income and supporting my family. Vocational training gave me a second chance." — David Mwangi, Carpentry Graduate
Young people developing practical skills at the vocational training workshop in Kibwezi

The Skills Mismatch: Why Academic Education Isn't Enough

In Kenya, the mismatch between academic education and labor market needs is stark. Every year, thousands of graduates leave university with degrees but no practical skills — and struggle to find meaningful employment.

In rural counties like Makueni, the problem is even more acute. Limited access to higher education, combined with a lack of local employment opportunities, leaves many young people trapped in a cycle of unemployment and underemployment.

From Theory to Practice: Our Vocational Training Program

The foundation's vocational training program is designed to equip young people with highly marketable skills that are in demand in the local economy. Participants choose from three practical disciplines:

  • Tailoring and Fashion Design – Garment making, alterations, and fashion design
  • Carpentry and Woodwork – Furniture making, joinery, and construction
  • Agribusiness – Sustainable farming, crop production, and livestock management

Beyond technical skills, the program also includes entrepreneurship and business management training, ensuring that graduates have the knowledge and confidence to start their own enterprises.

Real Stories, Real Impact

Case Study: David's Journey

David Mwangi, a 24-year-old from Kibwezi, had dropped out of school and was struggling to find his way. He had dreams of a better life but didn't have the qualifications for traditional employment.

"I never thought I could build a career without a university degree. Today, I'm a skilled carpenter, earning a steady income and supporting my family. Vocational training gave me a second chance." — David Mwangi, Carpentry Graduate

Today, David runs his own carpentry workshop in Kibwezi, employing three other young people from his community. He's not only supporting his family — he's also creating jobs for others.

Why Vocational Training Works

There are several reasons why vocational training is a highly effective pathway to employment:

  • Practical Skills – Learning through doing, not just theory
  • Market Demand – Tailored to local economic needs
  • Entrepreneurship – Graduates can start their own businesses
  • Shorter Path – Faster entry into the workforce
  • Community Impact – Strengthens local economies

"We believe that vocational training is one of the most effective tools we have for addressing youth unemployment," said Advocate Nzioka Kivuva, Executive Director. "It gives young people the skills they need to earn a living and contribute to their communities."

What's Next

Building on the success of the program, the foundation plans to:

  • Expand to 4 additional sub-counties by 2027
  • Introduce advanced specializations (e.g., welding, plumbing, electrical)
  • Establish a graduate support network and business incubator
  • Partner with local businesses to create apprenticeships

"We've seen the incredible impact of vocational training on the lives of young people," said the program manager. "Our goal is to reach every young person in Makueni who is ready to learn and earn."
