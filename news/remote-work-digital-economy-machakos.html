Beyond Nairobi: How Rural Youth in Machakos are Joining the Digital Economy

Discover how the Nzoka Kivuva Foundation is bridging the digital divide in Machakos, empowering rural youth with tech skills for remote work and the global digital economy.

In Kenya, the tech boom has largely been concentrated in Nairobi. But what about the millions of young people living in rural counties like Machakos? For too long, the digital divide has meant that opportunities in the tech sector remain out of reach for rural youth — until now.

The Nzoka Kivuva Foundation is changing that narrative. Through our digital literacy and tech training programs, we're empowering young people in Machakos with the skills they need to participate in the global digital economy — right from their home counties.

"I never thought I could work online. Today, I'm earning a living as a freelance graphic designer from my home in Mwala. The foundation gave me the skills and the confidence to compete in the global market." — Peter Mwangangi, Tech Lab Graduate
Youth participating in digital skills training at our Machakos tech hub

The Digital Divide: A Rural Reality

While Kenya's tech sector continues to grow, rural areas remain largely disconnected from the opportunities it offers. According to recent data, over 15% of youth in Kenya are unemployed, with rural youth disproportionately affected by a lack of access to skills training, internet connectivity, and mentorship.

In Machakos County, the challenges are even more pronounced. Limited infrastructure, unreliable internet, and a lack of awareness about digital career paths have left many young people stuck in a cycle of underemployment.

Bridging the Gap: Our Tech Program

Our digital skills program is designed to equip young people with practical, marketable skills that are in high demand in the global economy. Participants receive training in:

  • Web Development – HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and responsive design
  • Graphic Design – Adobe Suite, Canva, and visual communication
  • Digital Marketing – SEO, social media management, and content creation
  • Data Entry & Virtual Assistance – Administrative and technical support skills

Real Stories, Real Impact

Case Study: Peter's Journey

Peter Mwangangi, a 22-year-old from Mwala, had completed his secondary education but struggled to find stable employment. With limited options in his community, he felt trapped — until he discovered the foundation's tech program.

"Today, I'm earning a living as a freelance graphic designer from my home in Mwala. The foundation gave me the skills and the confidence to compete in the global market." — Peter Mwangangi, Tech Lab Graduate

Today, Peter works remotely for clients across Africa and Europe, earning a steady income that supports his family. He's also mentoring other young people in his community, paying forward the opportunity he received.

The Future of Work is Remote

The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated the shift to remote work, and that trend is here to stay. Companies around the world are increasingly hiring talent regardless of geographical location — creating unprecedented opportunities for young people in rural areas.

By investing in digital literacy for rural youth, we're not just creating jobs — we're building a pipeline of talent that can compete on the global stage.

"We believe that technology is the great equalizer," said Advocate Nzoka Kivuva, Executive Director. "By giving young people in Machakos access to digital skills, we're opening doors that were previously closed to them. The future of work is remote, and our youth are ready."

What's Next

Building on the success of our initial cohorts, the foundation plans to:

  • Expand the program to reach 200+ youth by 2027
  • Establish a dedicated tech hub in Kabaa
  • Partner with corporate sponsors to provide laptops and internet access
  • Create a mentorship network connecting graduates with industry professionals

"We've seen incredible enthusiasm from the youth in Machakos," said the program manager. "Many of them are already earning an income online. This is the kind of transformation we want to see across the county."
